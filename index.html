Kour.io is a fast-paced 3D first-person shooter where players compete in team-based battles across different maps. Each player chooses from various classes, each with its own strengths and abilities. The goal is to work with your team to outsmart and outshoot the opposing team. Players can earn rewards by completing challenges, which can then be used to unlock new skins and other customizations for their character. In addition to standard gameplay, Kour.io allows players to create their own maps and game modes, adding a lot of variety to how the game is played. You can also join or create clans to play with friends and adjust settings to match your preferred style of play.

Minecraft is a sandbox game where players explore and modify a world made of blocks, engaging in activities like building, mining, crafting, and battling mobs. The game features redstone circuits for automation, minecarts for transportation, and dimensions like the Nether and the End, the latter offering a final challenge with the Ender Dragon. Developed by Markus "Notch" Persson and later expanded by Mojang Studios, Minecraft's open-ended design supports both single-player and multiplayer experiences, fostering creativity and collaboration.

Roblox is an online platform that allows users to create, share, and play games, known as experiences, across various genres. Using the Lua programming language, players can design their own worlds, customize avatars, and interact with others in a highly social environment. The platform supports everything from obstacle courses and role-playing games to simulations and first-person shooters. Released in 2006 by Roblox Corporation, founded by David Baszucki and Erik Cassel, Roblox has grown into one of the most popular gaming platforms, with over 164 million monthly users as of 2020. Its expansion was fueled by user-generated content, a virtual economy, and a surge in popularity during the COVID-19 pandemic.
